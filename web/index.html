the feeling

Type into the suggestion. It won't fight you.

Most autocomplete flickers and rewrites itself every time you touch a key, so you end up reading instead of writing. Typer stays still. Type what it guessed and the grey just fades, one letter at a time. Change your mind and it quietly steps back.

You stay inside the sentence the whole time. It disappears into the writing instead of becoming one more thing to manage.

yours alone

What you write stays on your machine. Full stop.

No account. No upload. No "we value your privacy" fine print. Typer does its thinking right where you are — so the email you're drafting, the message you haven't sent, the idea you're still figuring out, none of it ever leaves the room.

  • nothing leaves your laptop
  • no account, ever
  • works with the wifi off
  • forgets you on command

It can pick up the way you write so suggestions sound like you and not a robot. Don't want that? Switch it off and it forgets you on the spot.

try it

One command. Then start typing.

Free, open source, and it sets itself up. A ⌨ shows up in your menu bar and you're going. 

# macOS 14+ · Apple Silicon
git clone https://github.com/frgmt0/typer.git && cd typer && ./install.sh

It'll ask for permission to watch what you type — that's the only way a helper at your cursor can work, and it's all kept on your Mac.
