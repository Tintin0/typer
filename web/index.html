you type the first half.
it shows you the rest.

a dim suggestion appears at your caret in almost any app and streams in word by word, with the first word on screen in under 100ms. take it, type past it, or ignore it. inference runs on your Mac, and nowhere else.

personalization

it learns you, not the other way around.

each app keeps its own session, so your group chat never leaks into your commit messages. a small on-device record of what you write (the messages you send, the suggestions you keep) tunes the model toward how you sound. it tracks how often you accept a suggestion versus type past it, so you can see whether it's helping. all of it stays on your disk and clears from the menu.

the lineup

one model per machine.

finishing a sentence is a small-model problem, and small models stay fast on Apple Silicon. typr ships three, sized to your memory. onboarding detects your Mac, recommends the one that fits, and downloads it on demand.

typer-1s0.6B

always-on safe on an 8GB Mac, where the system already wants most of your memory.

fits8GB +
balance point typer-1m1.7B

the sweet spot for 16GB. first word on screen in 27ms in our timing tests.

typer-1l4B

fits32GB +

any GGUF under 4B drops in if you want to experiment. typer-writer, a larger model for rewriting and drafting on request, arrives in Alpha 2. timing tables for every size live in research.

what won't change

two things, kept simple.

✓ free, forever

no subscription, now or later. typr is MIT-licensed and the source stays public. missing a feature? open a PR.

✓ local, always

autocomplete doesn't need your data. no account, no server, no telemetry. inference runs on your GPU through llama.cpp. airplane mode changes nothing.

install

about two minutes, one command.

$ git clone https://github.com/frgmt0/typer.git && cd typer && ./install.sh

macOS 14+. Apple Silicon recommended. grant Accessibility when asked. this is alpha: terminals and custom-drawn editors still get approximate caret placement. tell us what broke and we'll get to it.
